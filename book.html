Classic Novel Jazz Age American Dream

The Great Gatsby is a classic novel set during the Jazz Age. It tells the story of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire, through the eyes of narrator Nick Carraway. Themes include wealth, love, and the American Dream.

The novel is renowned for its vivid portrayal of 1920s America, its lyrical prose, and its exploration of the illusions and realities of the American Dream. Gatsby's lavish parties, unrequited love for Daisy Buchanan, and the tragic consequences of obsession make this book a timeless masterpiece.

“So we beat on, boats against the current, borne back ceaselessly into the past.”

About the Author

F. Scott Fitzgerald (1896–1940) was an American novelist and short story writer, widely regarded as one of the greatest American writers of the 20th century. His works chronicle the flamboyance and excess of the Jazz Age.
