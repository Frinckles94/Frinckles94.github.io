Aukstā Zupa - Latvian Cold Beet Soup

A vibrant, chilled soup that captures the essence of authentic Latvian culinary tradition.

30 mins (+chilling) • Serves: 4 • Rating: 4.25

Prep: 20 min
Cook: 10 min
Total: 30 min
Yield: 4 servings
Nutrition per serving:
180 kcal • 22g carbs • 8g protein • 6g fat

Ingredients

  • Beets 3-4 medium cooked beets (boiled or roasted)
  • Kefir 1 liter (4 cups) kefir (or buttermilk/plain yogurt thinned with water)
  • Eggs 4 hard-boiled eggs
  • Potatoes 3-4 small potatoes, boiled and chilled (optional, but traditional)
  • Cucumber 1 small cucumber, diced
  • Pickles 4 fermented dill pickles, diced
  • Onion 3-4 green onions or 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • Dill A generous handful of fresh dill, finely chopped
  • Seasoning Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • To serve Sour cream (optional), dark rye bread

Instructions

  1. Prepare If using raw beets, boil or roast them until tender, then peel. Grate or finely julienne the beets into a large mixing bowl. Dice the cucumber, pickles, and, if using, the chilled boiled potatoes. Chop the green onions and dill. Peel and chop the hard-boiled eggs.
  2. Assemble Pour the kefir into the bowl with the prepared ingredients. Stir gently to combine, ensuring the beets begin to tint the kefir a vibrant pink.
  3. Season Season the soup with salt and black pepper to taste. Mix well.
  4. Chill Cover and refrigerate the soup for at least 1-2 hours, or until thoroughly chilled. This allows the flavors to meld and the color to deepen.
  5. Serve Ladle the soup into bowls. Garnish with a spoonful of sour cream and a sprinkling of fresh dill, if desired. Serve cold, accompanied by a slice of dense, dark rye bread.
Tips
  • Beets
    For convenience, pre-cooked beets (vacuum-packed, unsweetened) may be used. Avoid pickled or vinegar-seasoned varieties.
  • Kefir Substitute
    If kefir is unavailable, blend 3 parts plain yogurt with 1 part cold water for a similar consistency and tang.
  • Make-Ahead
    This soup is excellent when made several hours in advance—or even the day before—for deeper flavor.
