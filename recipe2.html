Frikadeļu Zupa – Latvian Meatball Soup

A delicate and nourishing soup with small meatballs, potatoes, and seasonal vegetables, perfect for everyday meals.

Serves: 4–6
Prep: 20 min
Cook: 30 min

Ingredients

  • For the Meatballs
  • Meat300–400 g ground pork (or pork/beef mix)
  • Onion1 small onion, grated
  • Egg1 small egg (optional)
  • Breadcrumbs2 tbsp fine breadcrumbs or semolina
  • SeasoningSalt and pepper, to taste
  • For the Soup
  • Broth2 liters (8 cups) water or light chicken/vegetable broth
  • Potatoes4–5 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • Carrots2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • Parsnip1 small parsnip (optional), diced
  • Celery1 celery stalk, diced
  • Onion1 small onion, finely chopped
  • Bay leaf1 bay leaf
  • Peppercorns5–6 black peppercorns
  • DillFresh dill, finely chopped (for garnish)
  • SaltSalt, to taste

Instructions

  1. 1. Prepare the Meatballs In a mixing bowl, combine the ground meat, grated onion, breadcrumbs (or semolina), egg (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix until smooth.
    Using wet hands or a small spoon, shape the mixture into small balls, about the size of a walnut or smaller. Set aside.
  2. 2. Start the Soup Base In a large pot, bring the water or broth to a gentle boil. Add chopped onion, carrots, parsnip, celery, and bay leaf. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. 3. Add Potatoes Add the diced potatoes to the pot. Simmer for another 5 minutes until vegetables start to soften.
  4. 4. Add the Meatballs Carefully drop the raw meatballs into the simmering soup. They will cook quickly—usually 10–15 minutes. Avoid stirring vigorously at first so the meatballs don’t break.
    Add whole peppercorns and salt to taste.
  5. 5. Finish the Soup Once the meatballs are cooked through and the potatoes are tender, remove the bay leaf. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  6. 6. Serve Ladle into bowls and sprinkle generously with fresh chopped dill. Serve with slices of rye bread or a dollop of sour cream, if desired.
Tips
  • For a richer broth
    Add a small piece of smoked pork or use chicken stock as your base.
  • For kids
    Leave out the peppercorns and use fine semolina instead of breadcrumbs for lighter meatballs.
  • Freezing
    The soup freezes well, especially without potatoes (add fresh ones when reheating).
